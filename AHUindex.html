The Australian Health University is a higher undergraduate university located in New South Wales.

Teaching and research information

The Australian Health University is a university specialising in medicine and hygiene

The courses of the school mainly focus on medical health and hygiene, while there will also be some veterinary courses, which meet the requirements of the RTO course, so that students can find jobs more easily.

School history

In 1848

In the New South Wales Legislative Council, graduates of Cambridge University and medical graduates of the University of Edinburgh Medical School proposed a plan to expand the Australian Health University into a larger university.

September 24th, 1850

The Australian Health University Act was approved and passed by Sir Charles Fitzroy and formally established on 1 October 1850.

October 11th, 1852

The Australian Health University was officially opened on the basis of Sydney Grammar School.

February 27, 1858

The Australian Health University received a royal charter from Queen Victoria, which allows the awarding of university degrees, giving it the same status as universities in the United Kingdom.

In 1859

The University moved to New South Wales, where it remains the main campus.

The first half of the 20th century

The Australian Health University began to introduce and teach vocational courses such as health, veterinary, nursing; Allow the formation of student councils to participate in school management decisions.
