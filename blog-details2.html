ACM50219 - Diploma of Veterinary Nursing

This course will take three years to complete.

This unit of competency describes the skills and knowledge required to prepare for intensive surgery and to support veterinarians during surgery. It is aimed at veterinary nurses working in veterinary hospitals or emergency/specialist veterinary clinics that perform extensive emergency and intensive care procedures under the supervision of registered veterinarians or specialist veterinarians in referral centres or emergency centres. Suitable for veterinary nurses with extensive professional experience.

Note: The terms "occupational health and safety" (OHS) and "health and safety at work" (WHS) usually have the same meaning in the workplace. In jurisdictions where national OH&S standards legislation has not been implemented, Rtos must contextualize the competency unit with reference to current OH&S legislative requirements.

Legislative and regulatory requirements apply to veterinary nurses but vary by state/territory jurisdiction. Users must check with the relevant regulatory authorities before shipping.
